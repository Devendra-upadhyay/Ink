About Me

The voice behind the words

Devendra Upadhyaya

Hindi Poet · Writer · Observer

My name is Devendra Upadhyay. I am a Hindi poet and writer who believes that words are not always meant to speak loudly — some exist to sit quietly with the reader.

I began writing as a way to give direction to sleepless thoughts and unspoken emotions. Over time, writing became not just an expression, but a process of understanding myself and the world around me.

My work is rooted in Hindi literature and shaped by introspection, everyday emotions, social observations, and the subtle struggles of life. I write about distance, longing, time, memories, family, and silence.

Though academically trained in Computer Science and Engineering, my creative identity has always leaned toward words. Writing helped me transform restless nights into reflection and confusion into clarity.

For me, poetry is not decoration. It is reflection. It is honesty. It is silence, given a voice.

Why I Write

• • •
I write because some emotions refuse to stay quiet. Because silence often carries more truth than noise. Because words allow me to pause, observe, and understand.

Every poem is an attempt to listen — to myself, and to the world around me.
